Embark

Less than 3 percent of the world's deep waters have been explored. Most people never dive to below a depth of 20 ft. As a result, the vast majority of the deep blue sea is truly unknown. With Sea-SS, you now have the rare opportunity to travel to another world and come face-to-face with knowledge beyond imagination.

Join us on the mission of a lifetime, a dive that will illuminate places that have never known any light at all; places no human eyes have ever seen. Explore the Unknown. Go deeper.

Discover

Fluorescent Snapper

A rare species of perciform fish inhabiting tropical and subtropical regions of the ocean. Commonly seen in close proximity of the Tiger Shark. Mostly seen at depths of about 330 ft, but have been spotted at depths of up to 1,600 ft.

Crayon Ponyfish

A colorful species of seahorse, mainly found in shallow tropical and temperate waters throughout the world, living in sheltered areas such as seagrass beds or coral reefs. Usually spotted at depths of approximately 30 ft.

Electric Jellyfish

A species of jellyfish found in every ocean, from the surface to the deep sea. The electric species, in particular, is known for the effect of illumination created by the reflection of moonlight on their outer membranes.

Jaguar Shark

An endangered species of requiem shark and the only extant member of the genus Galeocerdo. It has only been spotted in temperate waters around the central Pacific islands. Little is known about the creature. Most infamous for having eaten renowned oceanographer Esteban du Plantier.
